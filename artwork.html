Artwork Gallery

AVILA


Piece I drew freshman year following a whole year of AP Art: from a picture I had taken before coming to Cal Poly.

CAREFREE


AP Studio art piece from a picture I had taken on one of Cal Poly's lawns near the upper dorms. 

FLIGHT


Drawn from a picture taken on a trip to Big Sur with my roommates Zac Saloff and Andrew Bold, as well as some others.

HEROES NEVER DIE


Piece I drew for the Danny Dietz foundation, in honor of the leader's son who passed away in his SEAL team's Operation Redwings on 6/28/05.

DANIELS PARK


Piece I painted while sitting in Daniels Park in Highlands Ranch, Colorado, while surveying the landscape. My first attempt at painting landscapes.

E-TYPE


Oil pastel piece that made it into my local county's art show. Drawn sophomore year of High School at ThunderRidge.

SADIE 1


Piece I drew for Art 101 at university of my dog, Sadie.

SADIE 2


Award-winning piece done my senior year of high school at ThunderRidge. Featured in the art show.

SYLVIA


Drawing of one of the main characters from the book I'm writing. One of my first forays into a cel-shaded graphical art style related to anime, attempting to stray away from conventional mediums to develop an artistic representation.

TSUNDERE SWAP


Piece drawn of two different characters from TV shows: Kurisu from Steins;Gate, and Stella Vermillion from RKNC. Further, stronger-effort attempt at mixing blending with cel-shading, distinguishing crisp line art from sketching, and differentiating between hard and soft boundaries for shading.

UNDERTALE


Developed from another piece shown below. The whole concept of the piece was built around Euler's spiral, as all the elements follow suit. Took several months to complete off-and-on until I left it unfinished.

STEINS;GATE ROSTER


Inspired from another piece I saw on the internet, I attempted to imitate the style in my own strategy of design. By combining textbook pages and backgrounds that corespond to each character, I attempted to characterize each entry.

GUTS


Inspired by this image taken from Berzerk, I attempted to add my own elements to the overall art style, since both characters hold thematic similarity.

WE CAN DO IT!


The idea for this piece came from a World War II poster designed by J. Howard Miller, and from efforts to practice using the Blender program.

S;G TRIO


Another piece from when I was hyped on S;G and interested in practicing cel-shading over conventional methods.

KAGUYA


Having practiced many iterations of cel-shading, I decided to attempt a realistic representation of a portrait with stylized elements and hard lines.
