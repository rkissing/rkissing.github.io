ALPS Project, Quality of Life Plus

Apr 2018 - Jun 2018

Project Lead

The objective of the ALPS, or Amphibious Leg Prosthetic Solution, was to design a prosthetic leg for a wounded warrior triathlete so that he could transition between running and fin positions in the middle of a race: a previous senior project that had been developed by a different team. Due to a faulty mechanism, our group's objective was to improve the transitional mechanism and optimize it for easy access.

  • Selected as project leader and managed 3 teams in analysis and redesign of adaptable athletic-use prosthesis.
  • Corrected faulty run/swim transitional mechanism through in-depth analysis, design, modeling, and testing.
  • Incorporated 3D modeling, development, and testing for buoyancy reduction, drag improvement, and completed project in less than 6 weeks.
